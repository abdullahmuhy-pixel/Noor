Nisab standard

Silver nisab (612.36g) is generally recommended by contemporary scholars for cash-based wealth since it's more inclusive of those who owe zakat. Gold nisab (87.48g) is the other classically valid standard. Both are legitimate positions — choose per your own scholarly guidance.

Your zakatable assets

For shares in companies whose core business isn't zakatable-neutral, some scholars require zakat only on the company's underlying liquid/inventory assets rather than full market value — a nuance worth checking with a scholar for large holdings.
Only debts currently due, not your full long-term mortgage balance, for example.
This tool estimates zakat on common liquid asset types using the standard 2.5% (1/40) rate on wealth held for one lunar year. It does not cover livestock, agricultural produce (which use different rates), or complex business/partnership structures. For anything beyond straightforward cash, gold, silver, and investments, or if you're unsure about your specific situation, please verify with a qualified scholar.
